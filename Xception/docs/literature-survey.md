# Literature survey

| S.No | Title | Author | Year | Main Summary | Brief Methodology | Evaluation or Results | Drawbacks |
|------|-------|--------|------|--------------|-------------------|----------------------|-----------|
| 1 | Can AI help in screening Viral and COVID-19 pneumonia? | Chowdhury et al. | 2020 | Investigated various CNN architectures to identify COVID-19 from viral pneumonia and normal X-rays. | Used Parallel-Dilated CNN, DenseNet201, and CheXNet with heavy data augmentation (rotation, scaling). | Accuracy: 99.70% (Binary), 97.9% (Multi-class). | High computational cost due to ensemble; limited dataset size led to potential overfitting risks. |
| 2 | Automated detection of COVID-19 cases using deep neural networks (DarkCovidNet) | Ozturk et al. | 2020 | Proposed a lightweight model for real-time detection on X-rays. | DarkCovidNet (custom architecture based on Darknet-19) with 17 conv layers; YOLO-based approach. | Accuracy: 98.08% (Binary), 87.02% (Multi-class). | Accuracy drops significantly for multi-class classification; lacks extensive external validation. |
| 3 | CoroNet: A deep neural network for detection of COVID-19 | Khan et al. | 2020 | Developed a specific Deep Learning model tailored for COVID-19 X-ray diagnosis. | CoroNet, based on Xception architecture pre-trained on ImageNet, fine-tuned on COVID dataset. | Accuracy: 99.00%; Precision: 98.6%. | High training time required due to Xception depth; dataset used was relatively small (approx 300 COVID images). |
| 4 | Automatic detection of coronavirus disease (COVID-19) using X-ray images | Narin et al. | 2021 | Compared multiple pre-trained ResNet variants for binary classification efficiency. | Transfer learning using ResNet50, InceptionV3, and ResNet101. | Accuracy: 99.70% (ResNet50); 98% (InceptionV3). | Used a very small dataset (only 50 COVID-19 images in early version), leading to high risk of bias. |
| 5 | COVID-19: automatic detection from X-ray images utilizing transfer learning | Apostolopoulos et al. | 2020 | Demonstrated the effectiveness of Transfer Learning on small medical datasets. | Evaluated VGG19, MobileNet v2, Inception, Xception, and MobileNet. Best results with VGG19. | Accuracy: 98.75% (Binary); 93.48% (3-class). | Specificity was lower for multi-class; heavy reliance on ImageNet weights which are not medical-specific. |
| 6 | Detection of Coronavirus Disease (COVID-19) based on Deep Features | Sethy & Behera | 2020 | Proposed a hybrid approach using Deep Learning for features and SVM for classification. | Feature extraction via ResNet50 + Classification using Support Vector Machine (SVM). | Accuracy: 95.38%; False Positive Rate: High in some tests. | Separate feature extraction and classification steps prevent end-to-end backpropagation optimization. |
| 7 | COVIDX-Net: A Framework of Deep Learning Classifiers | Hemdan et al. | 2020 | Created a framework evaluating seven different deep learning architectures. | COVIDX-Net (Ensemble/Comparison of VGG19, DenseNet201, InceptionV3, etc.). | Accuracy: 90.00% (VGG19/DenseNet); InceptionV3 performed poorly. | Extremely small validation set (only 50 images total); InceptionV3 failed to detect COVID cases (0% sensitivity). |
| 8 | COVID-Net: A Tailored Deep Convolutional Neural Network Design | Wang et al. | 2020 | One of the first open-source, tailored network designs specifically for COVID-19. | COVID-Net using a PEPX (projection-expansion-projection-extension) design pattern; COVIDx dataset. | Accuracy: 93.30% (Multi-class); Sensitivity: 91.0%. | Highly complex architecture requiring significant resources to train; lower accuracy compared to later ResNet papers. |
| 9 | Exploring the Effect of Image Enhancement Techniques on COVID-19 Detection | Rahman et al. | 2021 | Studied how image preprocessing affects Deep Learning model performance. | Applied Gamma Correction and CLAHE before feeding into ResNet/DenseNet variants. | Accuracy: 96.29%; F1-Score: 96.28%. | Enhancement techniques occasionally introduced artifacts that confused the model in subtle pneumonia cases. |
| 10 | COVID-CAPS: A capsule network-based framework | Afshar et al. | 2020 | Addressed the issue of small datasets using Capsule Networks instead of CNNs. | Capsule Networks (CapsNet) which use vector inputs/outputs to preserve spatial hierarchies. | Accuracy: 95.70%; Sensitivity: 90% (improved to 98% with pre-training). | Training is computationally expensive despite fewer parameters; required external pre-training to reach high sensitivity. |
| 11 | DeTraC: Transfer Learning of Class Decomposition | Abbas et al. | 2020 | Proposed decomposing class boundaries to handle data irregularities. | DeTraC (Decompose, Transfer, and Compose) model using ResNet18 backbone. | Accuracy: 95.12% (High sensitivity of 97.9%). | Performance is highly sensitive to the k parameter in the clustering phase; complex pipeline. |
| 12 | COVIDiagnosis-Net: Deep Bayes-SqueezeNet based diagnosis | Ucar & Korkmaz | 2020 | Focused on hyperparameter optimization for lightweight models. | Bayes-SqueezeNet (SqueezeNet architecture tuned via Bayesian optimization). | Accuracy: 98.30%; Bayes optimization improved base SqueezeNet. | Bayes optimization adds significant time to the training phase; SqueezeNet features are less robust than ResNet. |
| 13 | Deep learning approaches for COVID-19 detection | Ismael & Sengur | 2021 | Compared end-to-end DL approaches vs. Deep Features + SVM/RF. | Deep Feature Extraction (ResNet50) fed into Cubic SVM. | Accuracy: 94.70% (SVM method outperformed standard Softmax). | Requires manual selection of the best kernel for SVM; not fully automated end-to-end. |
| 14 | Deep learning based screening of COVID-19 | Mukherjee et al. | 2020 | Designed a shallow CNN to work with limited computational resources. | Shallow CNN tailored for limited data + heavy Data Augmentation. | Accuracy: 96.90%; Fast inference time. | Shallow networks may fail to capture complex, subtle patterns in early-stage COVID-19 cases compared to deep ResNets. |
| 15 | Deep learning based detection of COVID-19 | Jain et al. | 2021 | Comprehensive comparison of modern architectures on a cleaned dataset. | Comparison of Inception V3, Xception, ResNeXt. Xception performed best. | Accuracy: 97.97% (Xception); ResNeXt: 97.1%. | High false-positive rate in distinguishing bacterial pneumonia from COVID-19. |
| 16 | Use of Multi-level Thresholding and SVM | Hassanien et al. | 2020 | Utilized swarm intelligence for image segmentation before classification. | WE-MFO (Whale Optimization Algorithm) for thresholding + SVM classification. | Accuracy: 97.48%; Good segmentation results. | Dependence on heuristic optimization algorithms makes results difficult to reproduce deterministically. |
| 17 | Automated diagnosis of COVID-19 with limited posteroanterior chest X-ray | Punn & Agarwal | 2021 | Focused on diagnosing with very limited data availability. | Fine-tuned ResNet, InceptionV3, and NASNetLarge. | Accuracy: 98.00% (NASNetLarge). | NASNetLarge is extremely computationally heavy and slow for real-time inference on edge devices. |
| 18 | Deep-COVID: Predicting COVID-19 from chest X-ray images | Minaee et al. | 2020 | Created a benchmark using a large dataset (5000+ images) available early on. | Deep-COVID framework using Transfer learning on SqueezeNet, ResNet18, ResNet50. | Sensitivity: 98.00%; Specificity: 90%. | Specificity was significantly lower than sensitivity, leading to many false alarms (false positives). |
| 19 | A deep transfer learning model using GAN | Loey et al. | 2020 | Used Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) to solve data shortage. | CGAN (Conditional GAN) to generate synthetic X-rays + GoogleNet for classification. | Accuracy: 80.60% (4-class); 99% (2-class). | GAN-generated images can introduce artifacts that do not exist in real medical pathology; poor 4-class accuracy. |
| 20 | COVID-19 Detection using ELM with Pre-trained CNN | Turkoglu | 2021 | Combined CNN feature extraction with Extreme Learning Machines. | AlexNet features fed into Extreme Learning Machine (ELM). | Accuracy: 99.18%; Very fast training. | ELM is prone to overfitting if not carefully regularized; AlexNet is an outdated architecture compared to ResNet. |
